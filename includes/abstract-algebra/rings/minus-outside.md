Let $a, b$ belong to ring $R$.
Then $(-a)b = a(-b) = -ab$.

## Proof

\begin{align}
& ab + (-a)b
\\ &= (a + (-a))b \tag{distributivity}
\\ &= 0b
\\ &= 0 \tag{$0x = 0 \forall x \in R$}
\end{align}

Therefore, $(-a)b = -(ab)$.

\begin{align}
& ab + a(-b)
\\ &= a(b + (-b)) \tag{distributivity}
\\ &= a0
\\ &= 0 \tag{$x0 = 0 \forall x \in R$}
\end{align}

Therefore, $a(-b) = -(ab)$.
