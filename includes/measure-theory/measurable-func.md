Let $(X, \Ecal)$ and $(Y, \Fcal)$ be $\sigma$-algebras.
Let $f: X \mapsto Y$ be a function.

Let $T \subseteq Y$.
Define $f^{-1}(T) = \{x \in X: f(x) \in T\}$.

$f$ is said to be measurable iff $\forall T \in \Fcal, f^{-1}(T) \in \Ecal$.
